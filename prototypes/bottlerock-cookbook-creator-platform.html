Cookbook Integration

At the festival, people taste and watch food being made in real time. There's no video to save in the moment. Cookbook bridges the gap: find videos of those dishes later, save them, and recreate the experience at home.

Cookbook: The Content Narrative

From live festival experience to home recreation via Cookbook

Key Insight

Festival food is experienced in person. There's no video to save at the moment you taste it. The content opportunity is what happens after: finding videos of those dishes online, saving them via Cookbook, and testing whether you can recreate what you ate.

At the Festival

Taste an incredible dish at the activation. Film yourself eating it. React.

→

Back Home

"I need to recreate that." Search for similar cooking videos online.

→

Save to Cookbook

Find a video that matches. Save it to Cookbook in a few taps.

→

Cook + Compare

Make the recipe. "Does it taste like what I had at BottleRock?"

🎤

Content format: This naturally becomes a post-fest Reel or TikTok. "I tried to recreate my favourite dish from BottleRock. Here's how it went." Highly shareable, authentic, and demonstrates Cookbook's utility in a real scenario.

Section 02

Creator Platform

BottleRock attracts food-obsessed, socially active people. Some are already creators. Use the festival as a live scouting ground for HelloCreators.

Creator Platform: Recruitment Opportunities

On-site scouting + influencer-driven awareness

HF Team Scouting On-Site

Equip the HelloFresh team at the festival with a pitch card and QR code. When they spot someone creating food content or engaging deeply with the activation, hand them the card. "You should be one of our creators." Direct, personal, high-conversion.

On-Site Activation

Influencer Mentions

Brief influencers to mention the program organically in one story: "I'm here as part of HelloFresh's creator program. If you make food content, you should apply." Include a link sticker. Social proof from someone living the creator life at BottleRock is the strongest pitch.

Weekend Stories

"Apply to Be a Creator" Signage

Add a physical sign or card at the activation booth: "Love creating food content? Join 47 founding creators already on the platform." QR code links to application. Passive recruitment that works all weekend without effort.

On-Site Activation

Encourage Festival-Goers to Apply

Frame BottleRock as a moment where food-passionate people are already in "creator mode" (filming, sharing, discovering). CTA in stories or signage: "Already filming your food? Make it count. Apply to be a HelloFresh Creator." Meets people where they already are.
