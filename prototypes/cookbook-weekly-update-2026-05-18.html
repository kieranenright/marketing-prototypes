Key Learnings
1
Relatable static content drives the highest organic reach
Meme and "mirror" formats ("What's on your mind", "Adult vs kid problems", "Stop scrolling") consistently hit 8K+ reach. They outperform every other format on distribution.
8.1K avg reach 9.9K best post
2
Carousels earn the strongest engagement rate and are the only format generating follows
0.92% engagement rate (highest of any owned format). The only follow recorded across three weeks came from a carousel. Multi-image formats encourage deeper engagement and profile visits.
0.92% eng rate +1 follow
3
UGC product demo videos struggle organically
Vertical UGC clips (C3 through C6) consistently land at 1.3K to 3.7K reach with low interaction counts. Without paid support, short form video showing the save flow is not breaking through the algorithm.
~2.2K avg reach 19 avg interactions
What Messaging Resonates
4
"You already do this, we just fixed it" outperforms product education
Posts that name existing behaviour ("Stop scrolling and start cooking") or mirror a known frustration (saving reels you never return to) generate 3x+ the reach of posts demonstrating how the feature works. People engage with recognition of their own problem, not a walkthrough of the solution.
Pain awareness > Feature demo
5
Humour and emotional resonance drive shares, not product benefits
The two highest engagement posts were both conversational/humour formats rather than product feature callouts. People engage when they recognise themselves in the content. The winning value prop is "never lose a recipe again" framed as solving frustration, not "here's how our save feature works."
64 interactions (best) Humour/mirror format
Recommendations:
1. Lead the content calendar with static/meme formats for reach; use UGC video as supplementary or boost with paid.
2. Test more carousels: best engagement rate and only format generating follows.
3. Frame messaging around the problem ("never lose a recipe") not the feature ("save videos as recipes").
