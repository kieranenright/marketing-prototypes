What's Changing & Why
HelloFresh Rewards
is evolving.
The first iteration didn't resonate with US audiences. Customers want more choice, more variety, and more ways to be rewarded.
01

V1 Insight

The first iteration of HelloFresh Rewards did not resonate with US audiences. The level-based system lacked the engagement and variety customers were looking for.

02

User Need

Customers have expressed a desire for more discounts on base products and increased reward variety. They want choice and control over their rewards.

03

Business Hypothesis

"Missions" will reward users for completing specific tasks identified as early indicators of retention — ordering boxes, rating recipes, referring friends.

Key Shift

Moving from a passive "order and level up" model to an active "earn, choose, enjoy" model. Customers now have agency over their rewards journey — they decide what to earn and how to spend.

Key Message Pillars
Three pillars.
One voice.
Every communication leads with one of these pillars. Together they tell the complete Rewards 2.0 story.

Your loyalty, your choice

Emphasise customer agency. They choose what to earn and how to spend. No more preset rewards — the Reward Store puts them in control.

Example Copy

  • "Choose your own rewards"
  • "Rewards at your fingertips"
  • "Spend points on rewards of your choice"

Earn more ways

Missions expand earning beyond just ordering. Rating recipes, referring friends, and completing challenges all contribute to the points balance.

Example Copy

  • "Complete fun challenges"
  • "Earn points on every order, rating, or referral"
  • "Boost your balance"

Tasty perks, free to join

Low barrier, high perceived value. The program is free, easy to join (one click), and delivers tangible benefits from day one.

Example Copy

  • "Join today for free"
  • "One tap, endless rewards"
  • "Free and easier than ever"
