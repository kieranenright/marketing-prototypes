01 — The Strategic Insight

On social, inviting creators
hurts you. Here, it helps.

Every creator platform has a referral program. Almost none have a genuine reason for creators to use it. We do — because Cookbook's economics are structurally different from social media.

Social Media Referrals

Fixed attention pool

Every new creator dilutes the algorithm's attention. Their gain is your loss.

One-time bounty

Flat fee on sign-up. No ongoing relationship. No reason to care who you invite.

Quantity incentive

Spray and pray. Refer anyone — quality doesn't matter because the reward is disconnected from performance.

Vanity metric triggers

Rewards tied to sign-ups or follower milestones. Not actual content value.

Cookbook Referrals

Growing audience pool

New creators bring new users who browse and cook everyone's recipes — including yours.

Ongoing commission

Earn a % of your referral's recipe performance for 12 months. Their success is your income.

Quality incentive

Your earnings are tied to their success — you naturally refer people who'll actually perform.

Real cooking metrics

Rewards tied to recipes getting cooked. The thing the platform — and users — actually value.

The Core Positioning

Cookbook is the only creator platform with positive-sum referral economics. More creators = more recipe diversity = more users attracted = more people cooking everyone's recipes. A rising tide that actually lifts all boats.

02 — The Value Proposition

Invite creators you respect.
Grow the kitchen for everyone.

Three genuine reasons a creator would refer — beyond just getting paid to spam their contacts.

1
More creators = more cooks for you
Every creator you invite brings their audience to Cookbook. Those users don't just cook their recipes — they browse, discover, and cook yours too. A new seafood creator doesn't steal your audience. They bring seafood lovers onto the platform who then find your pasta recipes. Unlike social media, where attention is zero-sum, Cookbook's discovery is intent-based. More supply creates more demand.
2
Your taste becomes an asset
Who you invite signals your standards and your community. Become the person who curates the best corner of Cookbook. Featured referrer status, co-creator collections, shared visibility. Your reputation as a tastemaker — not just a content producer — becomes a platform feature.
3
Earn from the network you build
Ongoing commission tied to your referrals' recipe performance for 12 months. Not a one-time bounty. The better they do, the more you earn — passive income that rewards quality invites over spray-and-pray. Your network is literally a revenue stream.

Aligned incentives by design.

The commission model naturally filters for quality. You won't refer someone who won't publish good recipes — because your income depends on them succeeding. This means the platform grows with quality, not noise.

Key Design Decision

Commission is tied to recipe performance (recipes getting cooked), not vanity metrics (downloads, sign-ups). This aligns the referral incentive with the platform's core value prop.

04 — Messaging Hierarchy

One story.
Multiple angles to test.

The messaging framework for all referral-program touchpoints: in-app prompts, email, creator dashboard, and DM templates.

Primary Headline
The only platform where more creators means more cooks — for everyone.
Subhead
On social media, inviting other creators means more competition. On Cookbook, every creator you bring grows the audience for everyone — including you. Refer creators you respect. Earn when their recipes get cooked.
Supporting proof points
  • Non-competitive: users search by recipe intent, not creator algorithm ranking
  • Network effects: more recipe diversity attracts more users to the platform
  • Ongoing commission (not a one-time bounty) for 12 months
  • Quality-aligned: you earn based on their recipe performance, not just sign-up
  • Tiered rewards: the more quality creators you bring, the more you unlock

05 — Asset Mapping

How this maps
to deliverables.

The referral value proposition above feeds into multiple touchpoints across the creator journey.

In-App Referral Section
Dashboard integration
A dedicated referral tab in the creator dashboard. Shows unique link, current tier, earnings tracker, and referred creator performance.
Referral link → Tier progress → Earnings summary → Referred creator list → Share CTAs
Email / Push
Referral prompt campaigns
Trigger-based comms when creators hit milestones (first recipe cooked, first earning). Prompt them to refer while momentum is high.
Milestone trigger → Headline #1 or #3 → Single proof point → CTA to share
DM / Outreach Templates
Creator-to-creator scripts
Pre-written DM templates creators can personalise and send to peers. Focus on the non-competitive angle and shared upside.
Personal opener → "Unlike social..." contrast → Value prop → Referral link
Landing Page Section
Referral block on main LP
Replace current "Grow together. Get rewarded." section with revised value prop. Lead with the positive-sum insight, not the dollar amount.
